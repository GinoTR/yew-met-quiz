1. This passage is about Renewable Energy

Around the world, renewable energy has become a central topic in discussions about the future of 
our planet. Unlike fossil fuels, which release large amounts of carbon dioxide and contribute to 
climate change, renewable sources such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generate electricity 
without producing harmful emissions. Solar panels can be installed on rooftops, providing clean 
energy directly to households. Wind turbines, often located in rural or coastal areas, transform 
the power of moving air into electricity. Hydroelectric dams use flowing water to produce energy, 
and in some countries they already supply a significant portion of national electricity needs.

One of the greatest advantages of renewable energy is sustainability. Fossil fuels are limited and 
will eventually run out, but sunlight, wind, and water are naturally replenished. Another benefit 
is economic: once the infrastructure is built, the cost of producing renewable energy is relatively 
low compared to traditional methods. However, there are challenges. Solar panels depend on weather 
conditions and produce less energy on cloudy days. Wind turbines require specific geographic 
locations, and hydroelectric dams can affect local ecosystems. Despite these obstacles, governments 
and private companies continue to invest heavily in renewable technologies. Experts believe that 
renewable energy will play a decisive role in reducing pollution and ensuring a cleaner, healthier 
future for generations to come.

   A) Fossil fuels are unlimited
   B) Renewable energy produces fewer emissions
   C) Fossil fuels are cheaper to install

2. Which renewable source is mentioned as being installed on rooftops?
   A) Wind turbines

   A) It requires no investment
   B) It has low production costs after installation
   C) It eliminates the need for workers
   D) It increases fuel prices
   Correct Answer: B

4. What is a challenge related to hydroelectric dams?
   A) They depend on sunny weather
   B) They can affect local ecosystems
   C) They require strong winds
   D) They are impossible to build
   Correct Answer: B

5. What does the passage suggest about the future of renewable energy?
   A) It will remain a minor source
   B) It will replace fossil fuels immediately
   C) It will play a decisive role in reducing pollution

   Correct Answer: C

=== TEXT 2 ===
2. This passage is about Urbanization

Urbanization refers to the increasing number of people living in cities rather than rural areas. 
Over the past century, this trend has accelerated due to industrial growth, better job opportunities, 
and improved living standards in urban centers. Cities often provide access to modern healthcare, 
education, and transportation, which attract families seeking a higher quality of life. In addition, 
urban areas are cultural hubs, offering museums, theaters, and diverse restaurants that enrich 
social experiences.

However, rapid urbanization also creates challenges. Overcrowding can lead to housing shortages and 
higher living costs. Traffic congestion and pollution are common problems in large cities, affecting 
both health and productivity. Furthermore, the expansion of cities sometimes reduces green spaces, 
which are essential for recreation and environmental balance. Governments and city planners are 
working to address these issues by promoting sustainable development, such as building affordable 
housing, improving public transportation, and creating parks. Experts believe that urbanization 
will continue to grow, but its success depends on careful planning to ensure that cities remain 
healthy and livable for future generations.

3. What is one challenge caused by rapid urbanization?
   A) Increased rural population

4. How do governments and planners try to solve urban problems?

5. What does the passage suggest about the future of urbanization?
   A) It will stop completely
